# GPU Retirement Send-Offs

## Basic Business Formula

This is a high-touch retirement party planning service for early employees and senior technical leaders who have done unusually well and want a send-off that feels personal, tasteful, and easy for everyone around them.

The product is not "a party." The product is relief: someone gathers the memories, turns the career story into a polished tribute, coordinates the vendors, and runs the event so the retiree, coworkers, and family can actually enjoy it.

## Primary Buyer

Early employees, executives, senior engineers, research leaders, and long-tenured team members who have had a major career outcome and want a meaningful retirement celebration without making their assistant, spouse, or coworkers figure everything out.

Secondary buyers include spouses, adult children, close coworkers, chiefs of staff, executive assistants, and alumni groups.

## Two Party Formats

### Coworker-Only Tribute

Best for company-centered retirements, founder-era employees, senior technical contributors, and teams that need a polished but confidentiality-aware event.

Includes a career timeline, colleague memories, group photo moment, tribute deck, music cues, optional awards, giveaways, light food and drink, and a tight run-of-show.

### Family + Friends Legacy Party

Best for retirees who want the celebration to include the full human story: career, family, mentorship, hobbies, travel, volunteering, and the next chapter.

Includes family photo intake, a memory table, cake tasting and cake reveal, music, speeches, group photo, personal slideshow, giveaways, and a warmer social run-of-show.

## The Core Service Bundle

- Memory intake: short interviews, photo collection, career facts, favorite stories, and guest prompts.
- Tribute deck: polished visual story with career milestones, team impact, personal reflections, and photos.
- Cake brief: cake concept, flavor direction, visual references, inscription, serving count, and delivery needs.
- Cake tasting: small tasting session or curated tasting box before final cake selection.
- Group photo: planned photo moment, shot list, location, timing, and follow-up delivery.
- Music cues: arrival playlist, walk-up cues, tribute moment, cake reveal, closing song, and optional personal theme song.
- Giveaways: small keepsakes such as commemorative cards, pins, shirts, mugs, challenge coins, photo prints, or desk objects.
- Run-of-show: a timed event flow with roles, cues, speeches, transitions, and vendor arrivals.
- Vendor coordination: venue, catering, cake, photo/video, AV, printing, flowers, music, rentals, and day-of support.

## Better Party Ideas

- Memory wall with photos, patents, launch milestones, team screenshots, conference badges, and personal notes.
- "Rewirement" next-chapter theme with travel, mentoring, volunteering, learning, family, hobbies, and adventure.
- Reflections booth where guests record short video messages before or during the party.
- Signature cake reveal based on the retiree's career story, favorite flavor, or next adventure.
- Group photo moment with coworkers only, family only, and everybody together.
- Career soundtrack with songs tied to decades, projects, teams, and personal memories.
- Giveaway table with tasteful keepsakes guests actually want to take home.
- NDA-safe achievement cards that celebrate impact without revealing confidential details.
- Toast cards so guests can write memories even if they do not want to speak publicly.
- Post-party keepsake package: tribute PDF, photo album, group photo, guest notes, and music playlist.

## Launch Pricing

The first 5 parties are pilot events priced at actual event cost + 10 percent to build the business, prove the process, create portfolio examples, and learn what buyers value most.

Gratuity is always appreciated.

After the first 5 parties, the formula becomes:

Direct event costs + planning fee + optional creative add-ons + margin.

The planning fee should cover memory intake, creative direction, coordination, run-of-show, and day-of management. Add-ons can include premium tribute deck, video reflections, custom music, upgraded giveaways, photography, keepsake book, and expanded family intake.

## Simple Revenue Model

Pilot model: actual cost + 10 percent.

Standard model: direct cost passthrough plus planning fee.

Premium model: direct cost passthrough plus higher planning fee plus creative production add-ons.

Where money is made:

- Planning and coordination fee.
- Tribute deck production.
- Keepsake and giveaway margin.
- Cake and catering coordination fee.
- Photo/video package margin.
- Custom music or theme song add-on.
- Post-party memory package.
