# Plan: GPU Retirement Website

**Generated**: 2026-07-01  
**Estimated Complexity**: Medium  
**Primary Goal**: Turn the current single-page concept into a real retiree-friendly website that feels warm, joyful, trustworthy, and commercially ready.

## Overview

The website should not feel like a joke flyer or a vendor calculator. It should feel like a planning service for families, coworkers, managers, and retirement committees who want to honor someone with a real celebration.

The emotional promise:

> We help teams celebrate the person behind the work.

The commercial promise:

> Pick a celebration level, add the moments that matter, and request a quote without needing to plan the whole event from scratch.

The site should keep the GPU/NVIDIA-inspired visual language, but soften it with human details: photos, memory cards, cake, giveaway table, team stories, family moments, and a warm day-of flow.

## Launch Business Model

The first five parties should be offered at **cost + 10%** to build out the business. This is a pilot strategy, not the long-term price anchor.

Purpose of the first five parties:
- Validate real vendor costs.
- Track planner hours by phase.
- Learn which add-ons people actually buy.
- Capture approved photos, testimonials, and examples.
- Refine the website, planning checklist, and package language.

Gratuity is always appreciated, but it should never be required and should not be needed for the financial model to work. The business should make money through clear package pricing, reusable planning systems, vendor control, add-on attachment, and strong referrals.

## Core Audiences

### 1. The Retiree
- Wants to feel seen, not embarrassed.
- May enjoy the humor and GPU theme, but does not want the whole event to feel like a meme.
- Cares about friends, legacy, photos, family, food, and an event that moves at a comfortable pace.

### 2. The Team Manager
- Needs an easy way to plan something meaningful.
- Wants confidentiality and brand-safety.
- Needs budget clarity and a simple inquiry path.

### 3. Family or Spouse
- Wants the celebration to show the whole person, not just the job.
- Needs a way to contribute memories, photos, cake preferences, and guest details.

### 4. Coworkers
- Want simple prompts for what to say, what to bring, and how to participate.
- Enjoy giveaways, photo moments, games, cards, and funny-but-kind references.

## Recommended Creative Direction

### Direction: Warm Tech Legacy

Use a high-tech visual layer, but make the emotional layer warm and human.

Visual feel:
- Dark circuit-board base
- NVIDIA-inspired green and cyan accents
- Warm gold/peach celebration accents
- Photo/memory-card textures
- Subtle glow, not nightclub overload
- Less "GPU giant poster," more "premium celebration scrapbook from the future"
- A polished tribute-deck mode with video reflection cards, memory gallery thumbnails, and elegant gold-on-navy treatments.
- A lighter "rewirement" mode with compass/new-adventure motifs, portrait framing, achievement icons, and travel/giving-back/lifelong-learning themes.

Tone:
- Warm
- Respectful
- Playful
- Confident
- Easy to understand
- Never too corporate

Avoid:
- Too much jargon
- Fake claims of NVIDIA affiliation
- Overloaded pricing language on the first screen
- Making retirees feel like props in a tech joke

## Potential Risks and Gotchas

- **Trademark risk**: The site must not imply official NVIDIA affiliation.
- **Tone risk**: Too much GPU humor can make the retiree feel like a joke instead of the guest of honor.
- **Privacy risk**: Achievement copy must not reveal confidential work.
- **Buyer confusion**: A quote builder can feel exact when vendor costs are variable; keep "starting estimate" language.
- **Visual overload**: Circuit-board styling can become too busy. Use warm memory elements to balance it.
- **Audience split**: The buyer may be a manager, spouse, or retiree. Copy should work for all three.

## Open Questions

1. Should this service stay NVIDIA-specific, or broaden to "retirement parties for technical teams" with NVIDIA as one example?
2. Should the tone lean more heartfelt, funny, premium, or playful?
3. Do we want the retiree to be able to use the site directly, or is the buyer mainly a manager/spouse planning a surprise?
4. Should we eventually collect payment/deposits, or keep it as quote-only for now?
5. Do we want generated visuals, real stock-style photos, or a custom illustrated style?

## Rollback Plan

- Keep the current single HTML file as the source of truth.
- If the expanded version feels too long, collapse the Ideas Hub and FAQ into future pages.
- If the tech theme feels too intense, reduce circuit-board styling and lean into photo/memory-card visuals.
- If the pricing distracts from the emotional sell, move the quote builder lower and make packages softer.
